About BlockDAG

What BlockDAG claims to be

BlockDAG Network presents itself as a next-generation Layer 1 blockchain combining Proof of Work (PoW) consensus with a Directed Acyclic Graph (DAG) structure, claiming 10,000–15,000 transactions per second with near-zero fees. The project launched a presale in March 2024 and marketed three physical mining devices, a mobile app and a growing ecosystem including a casino, DeFi platform, staking and NFT marketplace.

BlockDAG's whitepaper was later found to contain text and formulas nearly identical to those of Kaspa — with some sections appearing to be direct screenshots. Kaspa took 8 years from inception to launch. BlockDAG claimed to replicate the technology in 11 months.

The people behind it

Gurhan Kiziloz
True founder — revealed December 2025

Hidden for over two years, Kiziloz was publicly revealed as founder after former CEO Antony Turner was fired. His previous company Lanistar was liquidated after the FCA issued a consumer warning for unauthorized financial activity in 2020. He has also been linked to Big Eyes, Dogetti and Poorcoin — all of which collapsed shortly after launch.

Antony Turner
Former CEO — fired December 2025

The public face of BlockDAG throughout the presale. Fired in December 2025 following the Alpine Racing payment default and the DL News investigation. Later revealed to have been a figurehead while Kiziloz controlled the finances.

Nic Van Den Bergh
Current CEO — appointed December 2025

Former Chief Marketing Officer promoted to CEO after Turner's departure. Community members have criticized his AMAs for failing to address missing miners, broken staking and delayed exchange listings.

The presale and where the money went

The presale ran for 450+ days. The project publicly claimed $442 million raised. The CEO later stated only approximately $200 million was raised. Blockchain investigator ZachXBT found less than $100 million on-chain. At an investor meeting on January 6, 2026, BlockDAG disclosed only $32 million was planned for post-launch liquidity.

Former payroll manager Liza Van Den Berg alleged the presale was oversold by over 100%. At least $110 million in funds was sent to accounts on Binance and BTSE via Middle Eastern OTC brokers. Leadership repeatedly declined to disclose where the funds are held.

Sponsorships and legal issues

BlockDAG signed sponsorship deals with Borussia Dortmund, Inter Milan and Alpine Racing F1. All three filed breach of contract or payment default notices after BlockDAG failed to pay:

Borussia Dortmund issued a €2,007,444 payment default notice in September 2024
Inter Milan filed a material breach of contract in June 2025 and issued a cease-and-desist in August 2025
Alpine Racing reported $1.4 million in unpaid sponsorship fees with a December 2025 deadline that passed without payment
Approximately 24 employees were owed over $140,000 for December 2025 salaries
